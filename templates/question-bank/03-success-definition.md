# Question Bank — 03: Success Definition

**Critical dimension.** Brief items here MUST NOT be `[open]` to clear contract for `briefed` phase.

## The win condition

- "Define 'this worked' in one number. Not 'users like it' — a number. 100 daily actives? 50 paying? 1,000 signups?"
- "If we hit your win condition in 90 days, what would you build next? If we miss it, what would you do? Both answers should be specific."
- "What's the minimum number of users / actions / dollars where you'd say 'this is working' — and how would you measure it?"

## Leading indicators

- "What's the earliest signal — within the first week of launch — that tells you the idea is valid? Not 'users like it' — a number you can pull from a dashboard."
- "What's the activation moment for a user? The action that signals they 'got it'? Track that, count it."
- "If you were going to bail on this idea, what would convince you? That's the inverse leading indicator."

## Lagging indicators

- "What's the 90-day metric? The 6-month metric?"
- "What's retention look like in your win scenario — D7? D30? Cohort retention?"
- "If the goal is monetization — what's CAC, what's LTV, what's the payback period?"

## Continue vs. kill

- "What's the threshold below which you'd kill this in 30 days?"
- "What's the threshold above which you'd commit another 3 months of work?"
- "In between those — what's the messy middle, and what would you do there?"

## Anti-vanity

- "Page views and signups are vanity metrics. What's the action that requires real intent?"
- "Don't measure what's easy. Measure what hurts when it goes down. What's that for this product?"
- "If you only ship one analytics event, what is it?"

## Pushback Triggers

If user gives:
- "I'll figure out metrics later" → push: "Strategist needs the metric in PRD. Take a swing now."
- "Users will love it" → push for the number
- A vanity metric (signups, page views) → push for engagement / retention / conversion
- "Whatever feels right" → push: "Pick a number you're willing to measure against in 30 days, even if you change it later."
