# Question Bank — 01: Problem Clarity

**Critical dimension.** Brief items here MUST NOT be `[open]` to clear contract for `briefed` phase.

## Who has the problem

- "You said `<X>`. Be more specific — name a real person, real company, or real demographic with at least three concrete attributes (age, role, situation)."
- "If this product launched today and was a runaway success, who would be the loudest fan? Name them."
- "Who's the worst-fit user — someone who'd hate this and not be your target? That helps me see who you ARE building for."
- "Is there a niche within the broader category you're really aiming at? 'Indie developers' is broader than 'solo iOS devs shipping AI tools'."

## What is the problem

- "If a user could only complete ONE task with this product, what is it? Phrase it as a verb-noun: 'edit playlists', 'find collaborators', 'schedule sleep'."
- "What's the moment in the user's day where they'd reach for this? Not the persona-level pain — the actual moment."
- "If I quizzed five real people in your target audience tomorrow about this problem, would they recognize it as a problem they have? If not, why not?"
- "Is this a 'painkiller' or a 'vitamin'? Both can ship; vitamins have higher distribution risk."

## How they solve it today

- "What's the current best alternative? Be honest — 'doing nothing' counts."
- "Show me — if I asked the user to walk me through how they handle this today, what's the actual workflow?"
- "Why hasn't the obvious solution worked? If the obvious solution doesn't exist, why not?"
- "Who's the closest competitor doing the closest thing? What do they do well? What do they do badly?"

## Cost of not solving

- "If this never gets built, what does the user do instead — for the next 5 years?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how much does this user actually care about this problem? Be honest. 5/10 is a yellow flag."
- "What's the budget the user would assign this problem? Not the product price — the time and money they'd spend solving it some other way."

## Why now

- "Why hasn't a smart person already built this? What's changed that makes now the moment?"
- "Is there a window? Will this opportunity still exist in 18 months, or is the timing tight?"
- "What capability — technical, market, regulatory — wasn't available a year ago that makes this possible now?"

## Pushback Triggers

If user gives:
- "Everyone" or "people" → push for named persona
- "It's obvious" or "you know" → push for specific scenario
- "It just needs to exist" → push for current alternative
- A long feature wishlist instead of a problem → push back to problem statement
