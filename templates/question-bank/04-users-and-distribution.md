# Question Bank — 04: Users and Distribution

## First 10 users

- "If you had to text the launch link to 10 specific people on day one, who are they — by name or by specific channel?"
- "If you can't name 10 people, what subreddit / Twitter list / Discord / email list reaches them? Specific URL or handle."
- "Are these users you can already reach, or do you have to acquire them first?"
- "What's the cost of acquiring user 11? User 100? User 1000? Roughly."

## Discovery

- "How do they find out this product exists? Be specific — 'word of mouth' is not specific enough. What's the actual mechanism?"
- "If you had $0 marketing budget, where would the first 100 users come from?"
- "Who's already speaking to your audience? Newsletter, podcast, community — name them."
- "What's the SEO play, if any? What's the term someone would Google to find this?"

## Try-once trigger

- "What makes them try it the FIRST time? Curiosity? Urgency? A specific painful moment?"
- "What's the headline / first sentence that makes them click? Try writing it now — 8 words max."
- "How long from 'first hears about it' to 'tries it'? <1 day = good. >1 week = bad."

## Return trigger

- "What makes them come back the second time? The third? The tenth?"
- "What's the user's reason to open this app on day 7? If you can't answer cleanly, retention is at risk."
- "Is there a built-in re-engagement mechanism — notifications, scheduled events, social loops — or do users have to remember it themselves?"

## Distribution channel proof

- "Has anyone else successfully reached this audience on the channel you're picking? Or are you the first?"
- "If you're first, what's the evidence the audience is actually there?"
- "What's your distribution edge — being early on a niche, an existing audience you have, a viral mechanic baked in? Be honest if there isn't one."

## Anti-build-and-they-will-come

- "If you can't articulate distribution, the idea is at high risk regardless of how good the product is. Want to take another swing?"
- "Would you rather have a great product with zero distribution, or a mediocre product with proven distribution? (Trick question — both fail. We need both, but distribution often gets short-changed.)"

## Pushback Triggers

If user gives:
- "Word of mouth" → push for specific source
- "Twitter" → push for specific accounts/communities
- "We'll figure out marketing later" → push: "GTM Strategist isn't here yet. Strategist needs at least a sketch."
- Distribution that requires a big audience the user doesn't have → flag this risk explicitly
