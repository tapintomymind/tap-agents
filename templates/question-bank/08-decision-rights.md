# Question Bank — 08: Decision Rights

## Final approver

- "Who has final say on product decisions — just you, or are there stakeholders?"
- "If a partner / co-founder / spouse / investor has veto, which decisions do they care about?"
- "Are there 'must check with X' triggers we should know about — pricing, brand, scope cuts?"

## Reversible vs. one-way doors

- "Which decisions in this project are reversible if wrong? Which are one-way doors?"
- "Stack pick is usually two-way for early MVPs but one-way later. Naming / domain / brand is one-way once launched. ICP is two-way but expensive to flip. Anything specific to this project?"
- "What decision do you most want to defer because it feels one-way?"

## Non-negotiables

- "Are there things you won't compromise on — quality of UX, ethical lines, technical purity, brand voice?"
- "What's the deal-breaker — the thing where, if Architect proposed it, you'd say 'no, never'?"
- "Any features you specifically want to NEVER add even at v2? Sometimes 'will not build' is a strategic choice."

## Speed vs. polish

- "Speed of shipping vs. polish at launch — what's the trade-off you favor?"
- "Are you OK shipping a 'meh' MVP if it ships in 2 weeks, or do you need to be proud of it?"
- "What's an acceptable level of jank for v1?"

## Public vs. private

- "Will you be public about building this (build-in-public, posts, demos), or stealth?"
- "Does the team need to be quiet about this product?"
- "Would you license this out, open source it eventually, or keep proprietary forever?"

## Risk tolerance

- "Are you willing to take on more risk for higher upside, or playing safer / proven?"
- "How much of your time/money are you willing to lose if this fails?"

## Pushback Triggers

If user gives:
- "I have no constraints" → push for at least one non-negotiable
- "Whatever you think is best" → push: "EA needs to know who's actually deciding"
- "Stakeholders" without naming them → push for who specifically
- "I want everything fast and high-quality" → push: pick one
