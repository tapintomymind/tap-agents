# Product Principles — Example

What "good" means to me, in product terms. Every entry has provenance for audit and retirement.

> **Format:** `- "Principle" — from <project-slug>, <date>`
> When the principle no longer reflects your actual taste, retire it.

---

## On Scope

- "Prefer narrow, opinionated MVPs over feature-broad v1s. A great single use-case beats a mediocre suite." — from `example-onboarding-rebuild`, 2025-11-12
- "Anything taking >4 weeks to MVP needs to be split or de-scoped. Long timelines hide unvalidated assumptions." — from `example-onboarding-rebuild`, 2025-11-12

## On Distribution

- "Distribution channel must be named (specific source, not 'Twitter') before scope-lock. Don't build in search of users." — from `example-podcast-app`, 2025-08-03
- "If the first 10 users can't be named or located via specific URL, the idea isn't ready for PRD." — from `example-podcast-app`, 2025-08-03

## On Quality

- "Polish at launch matters less than speed of validation. Ugly working > beautiful broken > beautiful unbuilt." — from `example-tools-cli`, 2025-09-21
- "If you're not embarrassed by v1, you waited too long." — Reid Hoffman attribution, applied 2025-09-21

## On Anti-Patterns

- "Don't build dashboards before there's something to dashboard. Metrics first, surface second." — from `example-onboarding-rebuild`, 2025-11-12
- "Don't add auth to v1 unless multi-user is in MVP. Single-user-no-auth is fine." — from `example-tools-cli`, 2025-09-21

## On Decisions

- "When in doubt between two reasonable options, pick the one that's reversible." — durable principle, 2024
- "Ship to a small named cohort first. No public launch as v1." — from `example-podcast-app`, 2025-08-03
