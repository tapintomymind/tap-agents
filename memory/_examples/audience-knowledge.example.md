## Solo indie devs / makers shipping AI tools

**Provenance:** `example-tools-cli`, 2025-09; `example-onboarding-rebuild`, 2025-11

**Description:** Independent developers, often solo, shipping small tools that integrate AI/LLMs. Aged 25-40 typically. Tech-fluent, design-aware-but-not-design-obsessed.

**Pains:**
- Solo capacity limits — can't build everything they want
- Distribution is the bottleneck, not engineering
- Burnout cycle: build excitedly → ship → traction is slow → demoralize → restart

**Discovery channels:**
- Twitter (dev Twitter specifically — accounts in their network)
- Hacker News (front page)
- ProductHunt (launch boost only)
- Indie Hackers community

**Try-once trigger:**
- Solves a specific itch they've hit themselves recently
- "I needed this last week" reaction
- Demo video or live demo > screenshots

**Return trigger:**
- Saved them time on a recurring task
- Plays well with their existing toolchain
- Doesn't require switching context

**What they hate:**
- Aggressive sales pitches
- Requiring signup before showing value
- Hidden pricing
- Subscription for occasional-use tools

## Music fans, ages 18-34, US

**Provenance:** `example-podcast-app`, 2025-08

**Description:** Heavy music consumers (~3hrs/day), early adopters of streaming, frustrated with algorithmic recommendations.

**Pains:**
- Algorithm pushes them toward what they've already heard
- Discovery feels passive, not active
- Hard to explore a niche without manual deep-dive

**Discovery channels:**
- TikTok (music discovery now happens in vertical video)
- Reddit (r/<genre> communities)
- Friend recommendations (still load-bearing)

**Try-once trigger:**
- Curiosity about a specific scene/genre they're getting into
- "I wonder what's hot in <X> right now"

**Return trigger:**
- Reliably surfaces something they wouldn't have found themselves
- Easy enough to use weekly without friction
